Kolkata

The city of joy

About

Kolkata is one of the most beautiful cities of India with its beautiful monuments. The capital of Kolkata in West Bengal. Previously, the city was named as Calcutta. It was the capital of British India for almost 138 years. The city is popularly known as the ‘city of joy’.

It is a place which mostly is dominated by Bengalis. Before the partition of India in 1947, Bangladesh or East Bengal was a part of Bengal province. The state language of Kolkata is Bengali. It is the place where the great writer and the Nobel Prize winner, Rabindranath Tagore was born.

The famous Shantiniketan is located in Kolkata. The largest National library of India by volume is located in Kolkata. It has become attracted to tourists from different corners of the country and from all over the world. The famous Howrah Bridge and Victoria Memorial and many other monuments have made the city a centre of attraction for the people.

Kolkata is also the city of many Nobel laureates like Rabindranath Tagore, Mother Teresa, Amartya Sen etc.

It is a city where various modes of transportation are available. The ancient trams are found in Kolkata. It is the first city of India where the underground metro services got introduced.

It is a wide place for shopping. It has become a centre for the businessmen around the country. People from different corners of the country visit Kolkata to buy different things at wholesale rate.

The city is also known for the biggest celebration of the festival Durga Puja. During Durga Puja, the city becomes a city of a huge crowd who join for celebrating Durga Puja. This time becomes one of the best times to visit Kolkata.

The whole city twinkles with millions of lights around the city. The place is famous for its delicious foods and desserts. Kolkata Biriani, Macher Jul and Kolkata Roll are famous in Kolkata. The desserts like Roshogollah, Mishti Doi, and Shondesh are very much popular in Kolkata.

Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial is a large marble building on the Maidan in Central Kolkata, having its entrance on the Queen's Way. It was built between 1906 and 1921 by the British government. It is dedicated to the memory of Queen Victoria, Empress of India from 1876 to 1901.

Princep Ghat

Prinsep Ghat is a ghat built in 1841 during the British Raj, along the Kolkata bank of the Hooghly River in India. The Palladian porch in the memory of the eminent Anglo-Indian scholar and antiquary James Prinsep was designed by W. Fitzgerald and constructed in 1843.

Howrah Bridge

The Howrah Bridge is a balanced cantilever bridge over the Hooghly River in West Bengal. Commissioned in 1943, the bridge was originally named the New Howrah Bridge, because it replaced a pontoon bridge at the same location linking the cities of Howrah and Kolkata (Calcutta).

Nandan

Nandan is a government-sponsored film and cultural centre in Kolkata, India.The primary aim of the cultural hub is to encourage and facilitate cinematic awareness in society. It includes a few comparatively large screens housed in an impressively architectured building.

College Street

College Street is a 900 metre long street in Central Kolkata in the Indian state of West Bengal. Also known as Boi Para (Bengali: বইপাড়া; lit. Book Town), it stretches from Bidhan Sarani road up to Bowbazar via MG Road crossing and Surya Sen Street crossing.

Rabindra Sarobar
